## 0. What Changed in v2

This revision reflects the team's alignment on the go-to-market model after investor-readiness review.

1. **Three clear B2B packages** — Starter (AI only), Growth (AI + Coach booking), Premium (AI + Coach + 2 Professional sessions).
2. **L2 "Coach" now means the school's own guidance counselor**, activated on our platform — not a kAIbigan-employed psychology graduate. This eliminates a major COGS line and flips our pitch to schools: *"We don't replace your counselors; we finally let them reach the students who need them."*
3. **L3 expanded from psychiatrist-only to a professional pool** — licensed psychologists, psychiatrists, and registered therapists. Engagement is pay-per-booking at a fixed per-session fee — no retainer, no bench cost.
4. **B2C marketplace layer** — any student in any partner school can self-book a professional at the professional's fee plus a kAIbigan commission (default 15%). Premium students get the first 2 sessions bundled; additional sessions use the marketplace with a Premium discount.
5. **Updated pricing** — Starter ₱50, Growth ₱120, Premium ₱400 per student per year. Reflects delivery cost differences across tiers and defensible margins on Premium.
6. **Updated financials** — new COGS math, new sales forecast, and a dual-engine revenue model (institutional subscriptions + marketplace commissions).

## I. Executive Summary

kAIbigan (a play on the Filipino word *kaibigan*, meaning "friend") is a dual-product mental wellness system purpose-built for Filipino students, early-career professionals, and the institutions responsible for supporting them. The company combines (1) a student-facing mental wellness platform with AI triage and booking and (2) an institutional-facing admin dashboard that delivers anonymized wellbeing insight, operational visibility, and measurable ROI — plus a counselor-facing workspace that lets school guidance counselors finally run a modern, centralized caseload.

**The problem.** Filipino youth face rising rates of academic stress and anxiety but are underserved by a fragmented mental health ecosystem. The cost barrier is real — but the *access* barrier is structural: DepEd requires one guidance counselor per 500 students, yet the effective national ratio is closer to one counselor per 14,000 students (Philippine Star, 2023). Even the counselors who exist are drowning in administrative duties, have no digital intake, no centralized scheduling, and no way for students to reach out anonymously — so they end up underused even where they exist (Davao City study, 2024). At the same time, schools and employers are expected to fund mental health support without clear, privacy-safe tools to measure student wellbeing trends, identify high-risk periods, or justify budget allocation.

**The solution.** kAIbigan is a mobile-first, B2B2C platform where schools and corporations fund access for their communities and receive a privacy-preserving dashboard that makes mental health outcomes visible and budgetable. Students sign in anonymously, optionally select a cultural preference for tone and framing, take a clinically validated intake (PHQ-9 / GAD-7), and are triaged into the correct level of care — AI, the school's own guidance counselor (activated through us), or a licensed professional (psychologist / psychiatrist / therapist) drawn from a partnered pool — with a Red Flag Protocol for crisis moments. Institutions get the reporting layer; guidance counselors get a modern workspace; licensed professionals get a patient-acquisition channel with zero platform fees until a booking is made.

**Goals.**
- **Year 1:** Onboard 5 pilot schools and 10,000 active student users across Metro Manila; activate guidance counselors on the counselor workspace at each pilot; 50+ professionals in the partnered pool.
- **Year 3:** 50+ school/corporate partners; 100,000 active users; Premium tier adoption at 20% of partner schools; break-even on operations.
- **Year 5:** National presence across the Philippines and pilots in at least two ASEAN markets (Indonesia, Vietnam).

**Target market.** Filipino senior high and university students (ages 15–25), graduate students, and early-career employees (ages 22–30). Sold B2B to universities, senior high schools, and mid-to-large Philippine employers.

**Competitive edge.** Five differentiators: (1) localized content and conversational tone grounded in Filipino youth experience, (2) a triage-first model that routes to the right level of care instead of dumping every user into a chatbot, (3) *activation* of the school's existing guidance counselors — we don't compete with them, we amplify them, (4) a privacy-preserving admin dashboard that gives institutions measurable, anonymized mental health insight without compromising student trust, and (5) a dual-revenue engine — institutional subscriptions plus a marketplace commission on every professional booking — giving us growth inside a single school even after the contract is signed.

**Team.** A product-led founding team with combined experience in software engineering, UX design, and market research, supported by partnerships with Filipino psychology practitioners and mental health advocates.

**Financial outlook.** Seeking pilot funding of approximately ₱3–5M to cover 12 months of operations: platform hardening, admin analytics development, content localization, partnered professional network setup, and go-to-market in Metro Manila. Revenue is led by institutional subscriptions (Starter ₱50, Growth ₱120, Premium ₱400 per student per year), a marketplace commission (15%) on every B2C self-booked professional session, and a B2C premium individual upgrade at ₱99–₱199/month for extended AI features.

## III. Products & Services

### Product & Service Description Worksheet

**Business Name:** kAIbigan

**Product / Service Idea**
A triple-product system composed of (1) a student-facing mental wellness mobile app, (2) an institutional-facing analytics dashboard for school and corporate administrators, and (3) a counselor-facing workspace for guidance counselors at partner schools.

**Special Benefits**
- Immediate, 24/7 support via AI CBT companion — no waitlist, no booking friction.
- Anonymous-by-default onboarding eliminates the single biggest barrier to student help-seeking: stigma.
- Clinically validated intake (PHQ-9 / GAD-7) matches severity to the right level of care.
- Content localized to Filipino youth realities: academic pressure, family expectations, identity, transitions.
- Institutional admin dashboard gives schools measurable, anonymized wellbeing insight.
- **Guidance counselors gain a modern workspace** — centralized scheduling, async messaging with students, anonymized caseload view, and visibility in a channel students actually use.
- **Licensed professionals gain a patient-acquisition channel** — no retainer, no platform fee, pay only when booked, fixed per-session fee agreed in advance.

### Level-of-Care Architecture (Revised)

| Level | Delivered By | How It Is Staffed | Who Pays |
|---|---|---|---|
| **L1 — AI CBT Companion** | kAIbigan product | In-house prompt design + clinical oversight | Included in every tier |
| **L2 — Coach (Guidance Counselor)** | School's own licensed guidance counselor | Free platform access for counselors employed by partner schools; activated via our counselor workspace | Included in Growth + Premium |
| **L3 — Professional Session** | Licensed psychologist, psychiatrist, or registered therapist from the kAIbigan Partnered Pool | Fixed per-session fee (₱2,000–₱2,500), paid only upon a completed booking | 2 sessions included in Premium; all other sessions are B2C marketplace |
| **Red Flag Protocol** | Automated + human escalation chain | Crisis hotlines + partner school emergency contacts | Included in every tier |

### Why Guidance Counselors (Not Our Own Coaches) Staff L2

This is a deliberate architectural choice, not a cost cut. The team aligned on this after reviewing the Philippine guidance-counselor landscape:

- **Mandate:** DepEd requires one registered guidance counselor per 500 learners; for senior high and tertiary institutions, CHED and the Philippine Guidance and Counseling Act (RA 9258) similarly scale counselor requirements to student population.
- **Recent legislation:** RA 12080 (2024) — the Basic Education Mental Health & Well-Being Promotion Act — expands mental health programs in schools and explicitly creates new school counselor positions, making this channel *more* staffed, not less, over the next 3–5 years.
- **Reality:** The real national ratio is closer to 1 counselor per 14,000 students (Philippine Star, 2023), counselors are overloaded with administrative duties, and a Davao City study (2024) found that student utilization remains moderate despite high need — with *limited awareness of services, no centralized way to reach the counselor, and stigma* as the top three barriers.
- **The gap we fill:** The counselors exist. The bottleneck is that there is no centralized, anonymized, student-facing platform that routes students to them. kAIbigan is that platform.

**What schools get:** Their guidance function, finally at scale — without hiring more counselors.
**What counselors get:** A workspace that respects their time (structured intake, async chat, triage pre-filtering so they see the students who actually need them).
**What we get:** Zero L2 labor cost. The school's payroll covers the counselor; our subscription covers the platform.

### The Partnered Professional Pool (L3)

**How it works.**
1. kAIbigan recruits and credentials a pool of licensed professionals — psychologists (RGC/RPm/RPsy), psychiatrists (MD), and registered therapists — each with verified credentials, expertise, clinic/hospital affiliation, and prescribing authority clearly marked.
2. Each professional agrees to a fixed per-session fee (targeting ₱2,000–₱2,500 depending on discipline and seniority). We pay them only when a student completes a booking.
3. Professionals list availability on the platform. Students book through the app.
4. kAIbigan's cost is predictable: a completed session equals a known COGS line item.

**Two-sided value.**
- **For the professional:** No retainer, no upfront commitment, no patient-acquisition cost. Every booking is incremental revenue, delivered through a regulated and anonymized platform they would otherwise have no way to reach students through.
- **For kAIbigan:** Variable COGS tied to revenue, no bench cost, no HR overhead for practitioners.

**Session delivery.** Secure Zoom / Google Meet links generated by the platform (in-app video is a v2 feature). Session notes remain with the professional per their scope of practice; anonymized outcomes data (session completion, follow-up booked, etc.) flows to the institution's dashboard.

### B2C Marketplace (Runs Alongside All Tiers)

Any student inside a partner school — regardless of tier — can self-book a professional session through the app. The fee structure:

- **Student pays:** Professional's session fee + kAIbigan commission markup.
- **Default markup:** 15% for students whose school is on Starter or Growth.
- **Premium markup:** 0% for Premium students who have used their 2 included sessions and want more. (This is our answer to "how do we keep Premium valuable without diluting it" — Premium students get the 2 included sessions *and* wholesale pricing afterward.)
- **What the student sees:** A single price on the booking screen. The markup is not itemized.
- **What kAIbigan pays the professional:** The agreed per-session fee on booking completion.

**Worked example (single booking).**
Professional fee: ₱2,500.
Student at a Starter-tier school self-books → student pays ₱2,875, kAIbigan collects ₱375 commission, pays ₱2,500 to the professional on completion.
Student at a Premium-tier school (after using 2 included sessions) → student pays ₱2,500, kAIbigan passes through, zero commission — the perk is baked into the school's Premium subscription.

### Why This Protects the Premium Tier (Addresses "Universal Marketplace vs Premium Dilution")

The team considered Premium-gating the marketplace entirely; we chose against it because the real mental-health gap is for students at Starter/Growth schools too — gating access would cost lives and cap our revenue. Instead, Premium stays valuable through four compounding perks:

1. **2 sessions fully included** — effectively ₱5,000 of included professional care per student per year.
2. **Zero markup after the included 2** — a ~13% discount vs Starter/Growth students.
3. **Priority scheduling** — Premium students see earlier slots; non-Premium students see 72+ hour horizons.
4. **Senior professional pool** — Premium students can book the platform's most credentialed practitioners; Starter/Growth marketplace defaults to the general pool.

### Unique Features

- **Stepped-care triage model** (AI → Guidance Counselor → Licensed Professional) with automatic routing by severity.
- **Optional cultural preference selection** — users can choose tone and framing preferences (Taglish, Filipino-first, family-aware, faith-sensitive) without compromising anonymity.
- **Counselor workspace** — async messaging with students, anonymized caseload view, calendar, intake pre-reads. This is a product inside the product, and it's the reason schools say yes to Growth/Premium.
- **Professional directory with full credential visibility** — license number, can/cannot prescribe, profile photo, name and credentials, expertise, years of practice, hospital/clinic affiliation. Students book an informed choice, not a mystery box.
- **In-app calendar UI for booking** — students pick a slot in a visual calendar (day/week views), filter by professional, filter by availability.
- **Red Flag Emergency Protocol** — keyword detection in chat and intake forms surfaces crisis resources instantly.
- **Mood-first sign-in** — the sign-in screen opens with a quick mood check-in.
- **Localized resource library** — aligned with each partner school's resources.
- **Anonymous aliases tied to verified school credentials** — we verify B2B eligibility without surfacing identity.
- **Institutional insights dashboard** — aggregate engagement, wellbeing, triage, and crisis-response metrics.

### Limits and Liabilities

- The AI companion is not a replacement for licensed therapy; all marketing and in-product copy makes this explicit.
- We do not provide emergency response ourselves — the Red Flag Protocol routes to national hotlines and partner school emergency contacts.
- Guidance counselors operate under their own professional scope of practice; kAIbigan's platform does not direct clinical decisions.
- Professionals in the pool operate under their own licenses; kAIbigan is the booking and payment intermediary, not a clinical provider.
- No parental access portal, by design: anonymity is the trust foundation.
- Not an EHR system; secure encrypted storage per the Data Privacy Act.

### Product and Delivery

- Native mobile application (iOS and Android) for students and early-career professionals.
- Web-based admin dashboard for institutional partners.
- Web-based counselor workspace for guidance counselors at partner schools (shares authentication with the admin dashboard; distinct role and view).
- Professional sessions delivered via secure Zoom / Google Meet; in-app video is a v2 feature.
- Content delivered via a managed, localized library refreshed monthly.

### Intellectual Property / Special Permits

- Brand and wordmark ("kAIbigan") filed for trademark in the Philippines.
- Proprietary triage logic and prompt-engineered AI persona — kept as trade secret.
- Data processing registered with the National Privacy Commission; DPO designated.
- Partnership agreements with professionals in the pool govern scope of practice, credentialing, and per-session fees.
- Partnership MOAs with schools govern counselor workspace access and data handling.

## IV. Marketing Plan

### 1. Market Research

**Industry size and trend.** The global digital mental health market was valued at roughly USD 20B in 2024 and is projected to grow double-digits through 2030. In the Philippines, the mental health access gap is well-documented: one psychiatrist per ~250,000 people and roughly one guidance counselor per 14,000 students (against a 1:500 DepEd standard) — a structural gap digital platforms are uniquely positioned to fill.

**Total target market.**
- ~4.1M university students in the Philippines (CHED).
- ~1.6M senior high school students (DepEd).
- Realistic 3-year addressable share: 2–3% of total students, or ~150,000 users across 50+ institutional partners.

**Trends.**
- Rising mental health awareness among Gen Z.
- RA 11036 (Mental Health Act) + RA 12080 (2024 Basic Education Mental Health & Well-Being Promotion Act) mandate structured wellness support and create new school counselor positions — direct tailwind.
- Corporate wellness spending trending upward.
- Buyer expectations shifting from "offer a mental health app" to "show us measurable wellbeing outcomes and risk visibility."

**Primary research (Google Forms survey, Q1 2026).** Distributed through Messenger group chats, student organizations, and class group chats. Targeted 20+ respondents across senior high, undergraduate, post-graduate, and fresh graduate categories. Key signals:
- Stress frequency (often / very often).
- Comfort discussing mental health (hypothesis: low → validates anonymity).
- Feature appeal: AI chat, mood logs, resource library, emergency button, professional booking.
- Willingness to pay ₱49–₱200/month for premium features.
- Willingness to pay per-session for professional bookings (self-reported ceiling around ₱2,500–₱3,000).

### 2. Barriers to Entry

- **Trust barrier** — users must trust a mental health app with sensitive data. Addressed via anonymity-first design and institutional endorsement.
- **Clinical credibility** — users and institutions expect evidence-based methods. Addressed via CBT-grounded AI, licensed counselor L2, and credentialed L3 professionals.
- **Regulatory compliance** — Data Privacy Act and professional scope-of-practice rules. Addressed with a DPO and explicit scope boundaries for counselors and professionals.
- **Supply of partnered professionals** — we need credentialed psychologists, psychiatrists, and therapists in the pool. Addressed by offering them zero-bench, pay-per-booking, patient-acquisition value.
- **Startup costs** — ML fine-tuning, localization content, dashboard analytics, counselor workspace. Addressed through pilot funding and phased rollout.

### 3. Threats and Opportunities

**Threats**
- Generative AI regulation in the Philippines could tighten, affecting the AI companion's scope.
- Larger global players (Intellect, Headspace) entering the Filipino market with deeper pockets.
- A high-profile incident (e.g., AI missing a crisis flag) could set back the entire category.
- Economic downturn squeezing institutional wellness budgets.

**Opportunities**
- RA 11036 and RA 12080 create direct compliance-driven institutional demand.
- Rising Gen Z demand for culturally relevant digital products.
- The two-sided marketplace — professionals want patient flow; students want access — creates network effects competitors can't easily replicate.
- Potential partnerships with LGUs and DOH programs for broader reach.
- Expansion to ASEAN markets with similar cultural dynamics (Indonesia, Vietnam, Thailand).

### 10. Pricing

**Pricing strategy: Value-based.** Institutional pricing reflects the outcome (counselor activation, fewer student crises, better retention, visible wellness ROI), not the cost of delivering an AI chat message. Marketplace pricing reflects transaction value.

**Institutional tiers (per student per year)**
- **Starter: ₱50** — AI companion + resource library + mood logs + Red Flag + optional cultural preference selection + basic dashboard analytics.
- **Growth: ₱120** — Starter + coach booking (school's own guidance counselor) + async coach messaging + counselor workspace + standard dashboard reporting.
- **Premium: ₱400** — Growth + 2 professional sessions per student per year (psychologist/psychiatrist/therapist) + 0% marketplace markup for students after the 2 included sessions + priority scheduling + senior professional pool + advanced dashboard insights.

**Marketplace (per session)**
- **Default markup:** 15% on top of the professional's fee for students at Starter/Growth-tier schools or any student self-booking beyond included entitlements (excluding Premium).
- **Premium markup:** 0% for Premium students who have used their 2 included sessions.
- **Student-facing price:** Professional fee + markup, presented as a single price. Markup is not itemized.

**Optional institutional upsells**
- Paid advanced reports for board presentations, accreditation, or annual budget planning.
- Benchmarking package comparing an institution against anonymized peer baselines once network scale supports it.

**Individual premium (B2C upsell — platform features, distinct from professional booking)**
- ₱99 / month — Expanded AI chat + personalized insights.
- ₱199 / month — Expanded AI + priority coach booking + premium content.

### Pricing Strategy Worksheet

**Pricing Model Selected:** Value-based, with a free core tier delivered through institutional contracts, a variable marketplace layer, and a premium individual upgrade.

**Rationale.** Filipino students have low individual willingness to pay for monthly subscriptions, but they *do* pay per-session for actual professional care when they need it. Institutions have strong motivation (retention, compliance, reputation, duty of care, RA 12080 alignment) to fund tiered mental health access. Pricing this way gives us (a) predictable recurring revenue from schools, (b) variable revenue from every actual session booked, and (c) compounding value as our professional pool grows. Premium is priced to cover the included professional session cost at realistic utilization (see unit economics below).

**Industry practice.** Competitors price on per-seat annual for enterprise and monthly subscription for consumers; none have a marketplace layer tied to the school's own counselor and a credentialed professional pool. Our Growth tier sits below MindNation and Intellect; Premium is higher because it includes 2 actual professional sessions worth up to ₱5,000 in service delivery.

### Premium Tier Unit Economics (Illustrative)

Pricing Premium at ₱400/student/year. Model assumptions (conservative):

- **Realistic utilization:** For each 1,000 Premium students, ~2% fully utilize both included sessions (≈20 students × 2 sessions = 40 sessions) and ~3% use one session (≈30 sessions). Total: ~70 sessions per 1,000 students per year.
- **Professional fee:** ₱2,500/session.
- **Professional-session COGS per 1,000 Premium students:** 70 × ₱2,500 = **₱175,000**.
- **Revenue per 1,000 Premium students:** ₱400,000.
- **Gross margin on session COGS alone:** ~56%. Adds to platform COGS (AI, cloud, dashboard) bringing total Premium gross margin to ~45–50% at pilot scale.
- **Worst-case utilization (20% book at least 1, 10% book both):** COGS = (100 × ₱2,500) + (100 × ₱2,500) = ₱500,000 on ₱400,000 revenue → tier operates at loss. Mitigation: hard cap at 2 sessions per student (already in place), buffer against utilization spikes via quarterly review.

The pricing is defensible at realistic utilization and capped by design against extreme utilization.

## V. Operational Plan

### 1. Production
Sprint-based software delivery (2-week sprints), CI/CD deployment to App Store and Google Play. Counselor workspace maintained as a distinct web app with its own access controls. - Clinical content reviewed quarterly by a licensed psychologist on retainer.
- AI responses audited monthly via red-team prompts targeting edge cases.
- Counselor workspace feedback loop — monthly interviews with active counselors.
- Professional pool credentialing audited quarterly (license validity, affiliation, scope).
- User-reported issues tracked with SLAs: 24h response, 72h resolution for non-critical; immediate for Red Flag.
- NPS measured per institution each quarter.
- Dashboard data quality audited monthly for metric consistency, anonymization integrity, and report accuracy.

### 3. Location
Metro Manila co-working space (~40 sqm for a 6–8 person team at pilot).

- **Licenses / permits:** Business permit (BIR, LGU), SEC corporation registration, DTI brand registration, IPO trademark filing.

- **Insurance:** Professional liability (for L3 professional pool bookings), cyber liability, general business insurance. Budget: ~₱180K/year.

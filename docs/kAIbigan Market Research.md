# kAIbigan — Primary Market Research

## Overview
Primary research to test the assumptions behind the kAIbigan MVP — a mobile app for student mental well-being with mood tracking, AI-assisted conversations, and accessible mental health resources.

**Assumptions being tested:**
- **Customer** — users experience stress and need accessible mental health support.
- **Product** — students find features (mood logging, AI chat, resource library) useful.
- **Market** — students are willing to use a mobile app for mental wellness.
- **Pricing** — students may pay for premium features.

## Target Respondents
| Category | Description |
|---|---|
| University / College | Ages 18–25 — academic stress, transitions, identity |
| Senior High School | Ages 15–18 — early intervention, school-based access |
| Early-Career Employees | Ages 22–30 — workplace stress, adulting challenges, limited company support |
| Graduate / Professional | Higher academic pressure; may have income for premium |

**Minimum sample:** 20 respondents.

## Administration
- **Channel:** Google Forms.
- **Distribution:** Messenger group chats, student org GCs, class GCs, direct messages.
- **Format:** Multiple choice, Likert scales, short open-ended.
- **Estimated completion:** 3–5 minutes.

## Key Questions (Summary)

**Respondent profile**
- Age range (17 or below → 26+).
- Category (SHS, undergrad, post-grad, fresh grad).

**Customer assumptions (mental health needs)**
- Frequency of academic stress (Likert).
- Coping behaviors (talk to friends/family, social media, search online, keep to self, other).
- Comfort discussing mental health (Likert).
- Agreement that students need easier access to wellness tools (Likert).

**Product assumptions (MVP features)**
- Usefulness of daily mood logging.
- Likelihood to use AI companion chat when stressed.
- Helpfulness of a resource library.
- Usefulness of mood trends on a calendar.
- Importance of an emergency help button.

**Market assumptions**
- Willingness to use a mobile app for wellness monitoring.
- Expected usage frequency.
- Device preference (smartphone, tablet, laptop, desktop).

**Pricing assumptions**
- Willingness to pay for premium if core is free.
- Price tolerance tiers: ₱49–99, ₱100–149, ₱150–199, ₱200+, or free-only.
- Premium features that would motivate subscription.

**Feedback**
- Most interesting feature.
- Suggested improvements (open-ended).

## Expected Outcomes
- Identify stress frequency and coping patterns.
- Evaluate interest in digital mental wellness support.
- Assess which features are most valuable.
- Gather improvement suggestions before further development.

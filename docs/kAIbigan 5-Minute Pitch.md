# kAIbigan — 5-Minute Business Pitch

**Total runtime:** 5:00
**Format:** 10 slides, ~30 seconds each
**Goal:** Leave the audience believing kAIbigan is the inevitable mental wellness platform for Filipino students — and that the team can build it.

---

## Opening Hook (0:00 – 0:30) — Slide 1: Title + Hook

**On screen:** kAIbigan logo · "Your AI Kaibigan for Mental Wellness"

**Script.**
> "Imagine it's 2 a.m. You're a college student in Manila. You have an exam in six hours, your mom thinks you're asleep, and for the first time in months you really need someone to talk to. Your guidance office is closed. Therapy costs money you don't have. And your friends are already stressed. *Who do you turn to?*
>
> Today, the answer is usually: nobody. We want to change that."

**Delivery note.** Pause after *"nobody"*. Then smile and introduce: *"I'm [Name], and this is **kAIbigan** — your AI friend for mental wellness."*

---

## The Problem (0:30 – 1:15) — Slide 2: The Gap

**On screen:** Three stats — one psychiatrist per 250,000 Filipinos · ~80% of students experience significant academic stress · Stigma cited as the #1 barrier to help-seeking.

**Script.**
> "The Philippines has a structural mental health problem. One psychiatrist for every 250,000 people. School guidance offices are overbooked. Therapy costs 2,000 pesos per session — out of reach for most students.
>
> And even when support is available, stigma keeps people from asking. In our own primary research of Filipino students, the most common answer to *'what do you do when you feel overwhelmed?'* was: **'I keep it to myself.'**
>
> That's not a product gap. That's a public health gap."

---

## The Solution (1:15 – 2:00) — Slide 3: kAIbigan in One Frame

**On screen:** App screenshot of the home page with AI chat + mood logs + Red Flag button.

**Script.**
> "kAIbigan is a mobile app that meets Filipino students exactly where they are: on their phones, anonymously, for free — funded by their schools.
>
> We deliver mental wellness in three tiers on one platform:
> - **Level 1 — an AI CBT companion** that's always awake, trained for Filipino context, grounded in evidence-based therapy techniques.
> - **Level 2 — human wellness coaches**, psychology graduates who take over when the AI isn't enough.
> - **Level 3 — licensed psychiatrists**, bookable through the same app when clinical care is needed.
>
> One intake. One anonymous alias. The right level of care, every time."

## Why Now / Why This Team (2:00 – 2:30) — Slide 4: Timing

**On screen:** Mental Health Act (RA 11036) icon · Gen Z digital adoption · AI capability curve.

**Script.**
> "Three forces are converging. One: the Mental Health Act now *requires* schools and workplaces to offer mental health support. Two: Gen Z Filipino students are already on their phones for five-plus hours a day — mobile is the medium. Three: AI is finally good enough to do real CBT, safely, with human escalation paths.
>
> This window wasn't there five years ago. It won't be open forever."

## What Makes Us Different (2:30 – 3:15) — Slide 5: Moat

**On screen:** 3 columns — *Localized · Triage-led · Anonymity-first* — with one competitor weakness against each.

**Script.**
> "You've heard of Calm. Wysa. Intellect. MindNation. They're real. They're also not built for us. Here's our moat:
>
> - **Localized.** Our content is built with Filipino psychology advocates for the specific stresses our students face — academic pressure, family expectations, identity. Headspace was built in London.
> - **Triage-led.** We don't throw every user at a chatbot. A clinically validated intake routes them to AI, coach, or psychiatrist — the right care, the first time.
> - **Anonymity-first.** We verify institutional eligibility but never surface identity. No parental access portal. Because trust is the precondition for honest help-seeking."

## Delivery Coaching Notes

- **Open with a story, not a stat.** The 2 a.m. hook is your most important 30 seconds. Rehearse it until it feels conversational, not scripted.
- **Use the word "kaibigan" three times minimum.** Opening, Solution (Level 1), Close. The name carries the whole brand story.
- **Numbers are anchors, not proofs.** Don't rattle them off — land them. Pause after each.
- **Never apologize for being early-stage.** "Pilot LOIs in progress" is strength, not weakness — you're building the evidence investors want.
- **End before the clock.** Landing at 4:58 looks confident. Landing at 5:02 looks rushed. Aim for 4:55.
- **Slide discipline.** One idea per slide. Three columns maximum. Words on the slide exist to reinforce what you said, not replace it.

## Brainstorm: Strongest Angles for the Pitch

These are the strategic bets baked into the deck above — use them to reinforce the narrative or substitute in if an angle isn't landing with a specific audience.

### 1. "Kaibigan" is the entire brand story
The name does what Western mental health branding can't: it feels like a friend, not a clinic. Lean into this in any audience that is Filipino or familiar with the market. *"We didn't name this app after a feature. We named it after what a student actually needs at 2 a.m."*

### 2. The triage model is our clinical defense
If anyone in the room is worried about AI giving bad mental health advice, the triage story is your answer. We route severity *before* we engage. Say it this way: *"An AI that doesn't know when to stop talking is dangerous. Ours does."*

### 3. Anonymity is a product feature, not a compliance checkbox
Most people hear "anonymous" and think of privacy regulations. Reframe it: *"Anonymity is the only reason a stressed-out student will open our app at all. Without it, this product doesn't work — so we built everything else around it."*

### 4. B2B is a feature, not a fallback
Some investors will push on consumer pricing. The clean answer: *"Filipino students don't have wallets. Schools do. And schools have reasons to buy that consumers don't — retention, reputation, compliance with the Mental Health Act. We're not avoiding consumers — we're building where the money already is."*

### 5. The Red Flag Protocol is what makes this responsible
If asked "what if someone is in crisis?", don't get defensive. Lean in: *"The Red Flag Protocol is the feature we hope nobody ever uses — but we built it with mental health professionals from day one, because when it matters, every second and every tap matters."*

### 6. The moat is localization, not AI
LLMs are commodities. Filipino-youth-context content, partnerships with local advocates, and triage trained on local culture are not. If you're asked "what stops OpenAI from eating you?": *"The same thing that stops them from beating Shopee in Southeast Asia — they don't speak the language, they don't know the context, and they don't have the trust we're building with schools."*

### 7. Policy tailwind: the Mental Health Act (RA 11036)
Turn regulation into a sales lever. Every school administrator knows they're supposed to be doing more. We give them a defensible, measurable, budget-friendly way to say yes.

## Anticipated Q&A

**Q: What if the AI gives bad advice?**
A: Two answers. One, we use evidence-based frameworks (CBT, mindfulness) and never position the AI as therapy. Two, the triage-first design and Red Flag Protocol mean the AI is never the only layer — severity always escalates to a human. We audit AI responses monthly with clinical leadership.

**Q: How do you compete with Headspace / Calm / Wysa?**
A: We don't — not on their turf. They're global content and generic AI. We're localized, triage-led, and institutionally distributed. They win consumers who pay. We win institutions that buy.

**Q: How do you differentiate from MindNation / Intellect?**
A: They're strong in corporate teletherapy; we're student-first. Our pricing, our age focus, and our AI-first entry point are fundamentally different. We're building from the education system up; they're coming from the enterprise down.

**Q: What about parental consent for minors?**
A: Senior high students (15–18) access via school-sponsored subscription, which includes parental consent at the institutional level — without creating a parent-facing portal in the app. That's deliberate. Anonymity in-product is what makes students actually open it.

**Q: Why not partner with LGUs and DOH from day one?**
A: Slower sales cycle, higher bureaucracy, harder to prove outcomes quickly. We start with private universities — faster decisions, clearer ROI — and use that traction to approach public institutions with proof.

**Q: What's the #1 risk?**
A: A high-profile incident where the AI misses a crisis flag. Our mitigation is conservative triage (we err toward escalation), clinical audit of AI responses, and the Red Flag Protocol. If that risk ever materialized we'd shut the relevant flow in hours, not weeks.

**Q: What's your unit economic story at scale?**
A: Gross margin expands as AI costs per user drop with scale and prompt optimization. Year 1: ~70%. Year 3: ~72%. The heavy cost driver is the coach and psychiatrist network — which is variable and aligned to revenue.

**Q: Why should a school pay for this when they have a guidance office?**
A: Because the guidance office is overbooked, not anonymous, and doesn't operate at 2 a.m. We don't replace the guidance office — we extend it. Our admin dashboard shows schools *exactly* how many students we supported that would never have walked into an office.

**Q: Exit strategy?**
A: Strategic acquisition in Year 4–5 by a regional health-tech platform (MindNation, Intellect), an insurer building mental wellness offerings, or a large HMO. Secondary path: continued independent scaling across ASEAN.

## Visual Design Checklist for the Deck

- **Palette:** warm neutrals + one confident accent color for emphasis (not the Red Flag red — reserve that exclusively for any visual referencing crisis support).
- **Typography:** rounded sans-serif (e.g., Nunito, Poppins, or DM Sans) — matches the brand's warmth.
- **Imagery:** Filipino student faces (with permission) or illustrated Filipino student avatars. Avoid generic stock imagery.
- **Slide density:** max 3 bullets per slide, max 7 words per bullet.
- **Data:** one chart per data slide, never two side by side.
- **Live demo (optional):** if time allows, a 20-second tap-through of the mood log + AI chat on a phone in hand is worth 1,000 words — but only if you have the time cushion. Don't sacrifice close timing for a demo.
